About Me

I was born in 2006 in Sharjah, and I grew up in a house that never had much. Eviction notices came often enough that they stopped feeling unusual.

On one of those days my uncle gave me an iPad, one of the first ones ever made. I remember watching YouTube with my brother, deep in Five Nights at Freddy's videos, when we came across something different: a fan recreation of the whole game, built from scratch in an engine literally called Scratch, shown as a timelapse. That video is the reason I do this. It's the moment I became a game developer, even if nobody called me one yet.

Every hardship after that had game development sitting somewhere close by. It was the escape that got me through things, and eventually it sharpened into a skill I could sell, a freelancer who could actually make a living from what used to just be a way out.

By 2026 life has gotten easier, mostly because of the work itself. Outside of it I train as a Jiu-Jitsu athlete, still trying to get better at that too. I'm hoping all of it, the games and the discipline, comes through in this application.

2024

Frontlines, and what I gave to it

This year belongs to Frontlines — not just what it became, but the specific work I put into it.

Five maps, one engine-first: a fully custom 3D-modeled character, in a game built on Roblox.

In November of that year I turned eighteen — and for the first time, my work could pay for itself. I began taking custom audio commissions, a hustle I built to keep improving while lifting myself up as a freelancer.

2019–23

Finding my footing, and my people

Somewhere in here it stopped being just practice. I was getting genuinely good at game development, picking up traction alongside a group of friends — together we built ClearlyDev, the first platform of its kind, specializing in selling Roblox assets. These years don't have a clean, single story. They're a dump of everything I was building at the time — places, cities, and worlds, some real, some not.

Nine years of teaching myself. Now I want to be taught. That's why I'm applying to AUD.
